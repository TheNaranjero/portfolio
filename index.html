Philosophy: My Approach to Data Science

Accessibility

It takes no effort to use colorblind-friendly palettes or fonts specifically designed for visual accessibility.

Simple Visuals

I prioritize simple, familiar graphs that can be understood by anyone over fancy but overwhelming charts.

Plain Language

Most stakeholders do not need a statistical lecture—they need a reliable answer that they can see and understand.

Features Before Models

Complex models have their place. However, for most datasets, creating a new feature leads to greater improvements than switching to the most sophisticated algorithm.

Data Has Context

Data is not generated in a vacuum. Understanding how the data was collected, when and where, which decisions and special situations took place helps identify bias and can become valuable sources of new features.

Diagnosis & Validation

Models have requirements to work, they are not magic wands that we can just use. Validation, diagnostic analysis, and testing assumptions to ensure that a model performs reliably in the real world.

Think Outside the Box

A dataset, a model have a clear limit, our creativity does not. We can challenge the boundaries of a problem: integrate external sources, create new representations, explore alternative methods, and experiment with the unconventional. Curiousity leads to improvement.
